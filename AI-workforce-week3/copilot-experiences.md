## Copilot Chat, Microsoft 365 Copilot, and Copilot Studio — how they differ

**Copilot Chat** is the simplest form: a conversational space where you can ask questions, think through ideas, summarize content, or get explanations without connecting to your work files.  
You would use it when you just need a smart thinking partner for quick help, learning, or drafting something informal.

**Copilot for Business (Microsoft 365 Copilot)** is where Copilot becomes truly useful at work.  
It sits inside Word, Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint, and Teams, and works directly with your emails, documents, meetings, and calendars. It always respect your identity and permissions.  
You use this when real job outcomes matter: preparing reports, answering emails, analyzing data, or catching up on meetings you missed.

**Copilot Studio** is different. It is for building. Building agents that do the work, any kind of work that it was designed to do.
It lets you create and customize your own copilots, define what they know, set rules, connect them to systems, and decide how they behave.  
You use Copilot Studio when you want Copilot to serve a specific business process, team, or workflow rather than just helping individuals.

In short, **Copilot Chat helps you think**, **Microsoft 365 Copilot helps you work**, and **Copilot Studio helps you design how Copilot works for others**.
